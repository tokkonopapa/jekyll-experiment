An honest question for Trump voters: can you blame us?

We’re not all going to see eye-to-eye, but we can still start to heal our country’s divides by understanding each other better.



I’m a liberal, and I’m in a bubble. I can admit it. I am also fortunate enough to have people close to me who are in different bubbles. So I’ve heard questions from loved ones who voted for Trump, as I’m sure many others have, that go something like this:

  • What’s the point in talking politics after the election?
  • Who’s mind do you think you’re going to change?
  • Shouldn’t Trump be given a chance to govern?
  • Why does it matter so much?

All I can say to Trump voters is that nothing about Trump and his presidency is normal. So what he does matters, a lot.

Before I explain further, I want to clear the air. No, I don’t think that everyone who voted for Trump is a racist. For the sake of argument I won’t even touch upon whether I think Trump is personally a racist. Or a sexist. Or a xenophobe.

I think we as a society need to get to a better place in how we talk to each other. I suggest we start off by agreeing to a number of assumptions about each other. So here’s what I assume: I assume you are a decent person and you care about our country. I assume you discuss politics in good faith and try to be as respectful, empathetic, open-minded, and self-reflective as you can. In turn I hope that you will make the same assumptions of me.

So here’s the thing: I’m coming to realize that the things about Trump that I’m most afraid of are the things that most strongly drew people to him. Things like utilizing incendiary rhetoric, fanning the flames of racial animus, inciting violence against protesters, belittling and restricting journalists, promising to jail his political opponent, even joking about her being assassinated.

I know you probably don’t see these as terribly worrisome things. If you did, you wouldn’t have voted for Trump. Here’s where I’ll get more candid than I usually do: I honestly believe that there is a high chance Trump will cause chaos, destruction, and humanitarian loss on a scale beyond any other modern American president. Sorry if that’s a bit of a downer. If it’s any consolation, that spiel hasn’t been a hit at parties.

I know my viewpoint is considered alarmist by many Trump voters. But realize that we just finished a very long campaign. A campaign that was filled with Trump controversies on a daily basis. Time after time Trump said something that liberals were sure would sink him, and time after time Trump’s supporters stayed with him. So when you combine the fact that we liberals have real concerns about what Trump will try to do, and his supporters’ unflinching support… Yeah, we’re alarmed.

I understand that you do not agree with me when I say that Trump is dangerous. But I want you to at least understand why I feel that way. I hope you can connect the dots between the evidence I see and the conclusions I draw. You feel differently than I do because you interpret the evidence differently. I get that. But I want you to acknowledge that it’s not illogical for someone who interprets the evidence differently to be very very worried right now about the future of the country and the planet.

I would welcome some evidence that Trump is putting aside his strongman traits and trying to mend the nation. Because all I’ve seen since Election Day is more of the same. He has called those who opposed his campaign his enemies. He continues to make dishonest and misleading statements. His reaction to Russia’s involvement in the campaign email hacks has been lukewarm. He still hasn’t released his tax returns. He tries to intimidate and silence the press. He refuses to earnestly address his business conflicts. He’s shown his nepotism by giving his son-in-law a senior position. His cabinet picks either want to dismantle the departments they will be leading or are wholly unqualified for the job (or both). He advocates torture. He has suggested he would start another nuclear arms race.

Give me anything that Trump has done with the earnest intention of putting my mind at ease. Because until he does, we anti-Trumpers will continue to act under the assumption that he is headed down the path of an authoritarian demagogue. The fact that many voters applaud his rhetoric and cheer him on as he breaks our political norms, one after the other, is what makes this feel like a fever dream to us. Should liberals just chalk it up to hyper-partisanship, or a cult of personality?

If our shared goal is to heal the divides in our country, we should all push Trump to moderate himself and act in a more conciliatory manner. We’d all be better off if we could trust Trump and give him the benefit of the doubt. If Trump wants our trust, though, he needs to admit what he’s done to create distrust and start to reverse his course.

So I’m actually left wondering why the onus is on liberals to explain to conservatives the danger of Trump. The evidence is there, the norms are there. It should be totally understandable to be worried.

What I really should be asking is, why isn’t the onus on conservatives to explain to liberals why we shouldn’t be worried?

Here’s the sad answer: because Democrats don’t have power anymore. They can filibuster in the Senate and file lawsuits, but that’s about it. It doesn’t matter if they’re worried or not.

So, we have to make it matter. And it starts with being vocal. You could say that either I’m preaching to the choir or my words are falling on deaf ears when I criticize Trump. But from my view I’m helping to energize like-minded individuals and making inroads with Trump voters who might be having second thoughts. That seems totally worth it to me.

Lastly, let me just say this: if we’re being honest, y’all picked Trump partly because we hated him so much. I mean, he really is anathema to Democrats, and frankly to many Republicans and independents. He was a big middle finger to the political class that had written you off. And now it’s your turn to write off the haters. I’m asking you not to.

I know you care about the same things that the anti-Trump crowd cares about. Things like democracy, civil rights, and economic opportunity. Instead of downplaying their complaints, I hope you will let liberals know that you get where they’re coming from. Even if you disagree with them completely.
